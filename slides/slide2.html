The Problem

Logistics in Kenya is Broken for the Many.

Current solutions are built for large enterprises, leaving small merchants and everyday households behind with prohibitive costs and complexity.

Prohibitive Costs & Fees

Existing logistics providers layer on subscription fees and high per-delivery charges. This pricing model makes professional logistics impossible for small businesses and low-income households to afford.

Trust Deficit & Friction

Without reliable tracking or bundling services, customers fear theft and delays. The "stress of transportation" falls entirely on the merchant, creating significant friction in every transaction.

Remote Areas Underserved

Most logistics networks stop at major city limits. Remote and rural areas are neglected despite high demand for agricultural and general goods transport, deepening the economic divide.
